Krape Park

Disc Golf Course

Krape Park Disc Golf Course, installed in 2003, is a 19 hole park-style course that flows through beautiful Krape Park on the westside of Freeport, IL. Despite its beauty, it can pose a challenge for all but the best of disc golfers! However, the course is still forgiving enough to be great for people new to the sport or people that just enjoy throwing discs while taking a beautiful stroll through the park. Krape Park has become the home of two area tournament: Krape Park Doubles Presented by Kronk Disc Golf and the Krape Park Battle of the Birdies, a PDGA-sanctioned C-Tier event.

Krape Park Disc Golf course features several challenging wooded holes, holes with water hazards, tight fairways, and has several holes that allow players to put a good rip on a disc. Hole 11 is the hole to watch for! At just 200 feet, it seems easy enough, but with a fairway covered in trees and surrounded by out-of-bounds and thick brush, this hole can easily become a round-ruiner as well as a disc eater.

The course offers multiple trash cans along the route, passes many benches and picnic tables, and has easy bathroom accessibility for the 12 holes at the top of Flagstaff Hill. The bathrooms are most accessible by holes 1, 7,8, and hole 9's teepad. Be aware that this is a multi-use park, so you may find cars parked near fairways, people picnicing in fairways, walkers walking on the walking paths on and near the course, people setting up to fish on the coure, and cars driving on the road that winds through the course. Please do not attempt any shots that could put someone in danger.

Location

Krape Park Disc Golf Course is located at Flagstaff Hill inside Krape Park.
